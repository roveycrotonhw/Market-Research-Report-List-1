<p><p>Connectivity platforms for IoT devices can be classified into two main types: cellular and non-cellular connectivity. Cellular connectivity utilizes mobile networks (like LTE and 5G) to enable widespread, high-speed communication over long distances, ideal for devices needing real-time data transfer. In contrast, non-cellular connectivity includes technologies such as Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and LPWAN, which are suited for short-range, low-power, and often less expensive connections, offering flexibility for various applications, from smart homes to industrial automation.</p></p>
